week 01 - Graphics of Data

  1. Anscombe's Quartet (WEB PAGE)

    Francis Anscombe's demonstration of the importance of visualizing data from his 1973 paper 'Graphs in Statistical Analysis'.

  2. Datasaurus Dozen (WEB PAGE)

    Matejka & Fitzmaurice take Anscombe's ideas even further, showing just how different data sets can be even when summary statistics are identical. Have a look at Figure 8, which shows variation in a single variable.

  3. Cornell Notes (WEB PAGE)

    The Cornell Notes method for note-taking in lectures is worth considering.
    The approach has helped some students in the past and seems useful to me.

  4. The Cornell Note Taking System (MOVIE)

    It is explained in two short videos on the The Cornell Note Taking System.
    Taking notes like this in lectures will give us some pointers for discussion - we have time to for discussion through which we can explore the concepts, issues involved in visualizing society.
    IMPORTANT: slides that I use in class will not contain detailed notes of explanations, but PDFs will be available for you to download and annotate shortly before classes. So make sure that you have a good system for storing and annotating PDFs and linking the slides and your notes.

    The Cornell Note Taking System
  5. giCentre Research (WEB PAGE)

    Have a browse around to find out the kind of visualization we do in the giCentre research group.

  6. Two Quantities (WEB PAGE)

    Santiago Ortiz's exercise in which he presents 45 different ways to represent two quantities with graphics.

  7. Jason Dykes - Google Scholar (WEB PAGE)

    Google's list of publications that I have authored shows the kinds of things that I publish about and identifies more than 5,000 citations to my work over the years.

  8. IEEE VIS (WEB PAGE)

    Much of the best research undertaken at the giCentre is published at IEEE VIS - the world's leading forum for visualization research.

  9. Active Reading (MOVIE)

    An introduction to the paper on Active Reading by Jagoda Walny and colleagues. They show that actively working with a visualization can improve performance. I try to use this research to persuade you to actively annotate your notes! The visualization is about relationships within a social group.

    Active Reading

    Job search methods among unemployed job seekers, Estonia, 2008 - an example graphic from Our World in Data!

  11. Brexit Maps & Analysis (WEB PAGE)

    Roger Beecham's work on locally varying explanations behind the Brexit vote
